The History of Donuts

  • Orgins

    The doughnuts originated from ancient Egyptions frying dough to make snacks similar to cakes. These were fed to Pharaohs and the wealthy.

    6000 years ago

  • Sweet doughnuts

    Honey gets added to doughnuts in Greece and Rome. These became Olympic prizes in Greece, and in Rome it became a very popular festival treat.

    Cato the Elder had a recipe for these honey cakes in his cookbook"De Agri Cultura".That recipe is considered to be the precursor of dounuts

  • North America

    The dutch end up bringing donuts,or what they called olykoeks, to North America.

    These are bassically the modern day donutholes,aside from also being filled with nuts and dried fruits.

  • Modern donut shape

    Hanson Gregory, a ship captain, is credited with inventing the modern donut shape by punching a hole in the center of the dough to ensure even cooking.

    This cooking technique spread and by 1877, cookbooks had this technique.

    1847/1877

  • WWI

    Donuts were sent to France by woman as a treat for the solders. These people would be nicknamed Doughnut Girls/Donut Lassies and doughboy solders.

    This did a good job of spreading donuts popularity outside the US, and kept solders wanting more when they returned.

    This is how National Donut Day came to be to honor the work of the Donut Lassies, which is June 7th.

    1914-1918

  • Donut Machine

    The invention of the automated donut machine by a Russian named Adolph Levitt in 1920 revolutionized donut production, making it faster and more efficient.

    This idea would spread thoughout New York and would become a speticle to watch the machine.

    This machine would get donuts called the food hit of the centry at the 1934 World Fair in Chicago

    1920

  • Krispy Kreme

    Krispy Kreme's orgin comes from a Frenchman named Joe Lebeau, who had secret recipe that was given to Ishmael Armstrong.

    He then hired Vernon Rudolph to sell these Krispy Kream treats.

    In 1937,Vernon would then combined potatoes,sugar, and milk with the treats, and the Krispy Kreme donut was born.

    1937

  • WWII

    During WWII,donuts were again sent to solders as a treat by the Salvation Army and Red Cross, continuing the tradition from WWI.

    1938-1945

    In 1950,he renamed the shop to Dunkin'Donuts,emphasizing the dunking of donuts in coffee, donuts sold for 5 cent, 10 cents for coffee.
